# E1 — Promotion-probability sensitivity sweep

## Setup

For each of three workloads, sweep the joint promotion-chance knob (`PROMO_OPT = PROMO_SHARED = PROMO_EXCL = N`) over `N ∈ {0, 16, 32, 128}`, holding `PROMO_DISPLACE = 16`. Two replicates per cell. In-memory regime (pool larger than working set throughout the 25 s run).

## Results

| | TPC-C (k TX/s) | Random Read (k TX/s) | Skewed YCSB (k TX/s) |
|---|---|---|---|
| chance = ∞ (no promote) | 633 (−1.7 %) | 15 521 (−1.7 %) | 25 394 (−4.4 %) |
| chance = 1/16 | **651 (+1.2 %)** | **16 033 (+1.6 %)** | 25 579 (−3.7 %) |
| chance = 1/32 (paper default) | 644 | 15 786 | **26 552** |
| chance = 1/128 | 622 (−3.3 %) | 16 016 (+1.5 %) | 26 014 (−2.0 %) |

## Verdict

**Supports claim:C1** — workload-dependent optima: TPC-C and Random Read peak at 1/16, Skewed YCSB at 1/32. The paper's 1/32 default is the optimum for the skewed-read regime it highlights, but mildly suboptimal elsewhere.

Δ on TPC-C is within one σ (σ ≈ 35 k vs Δ ≈ 8 k) — marked as *suggestive*, not definitive. Directionality is consistent across both replicates.
