# E2 — CBP-v3 comparison (round 1)

## Setup

For each workload, compare four configurations:
- **Traditional** — `DISABLE_PRED=1`, predictive translation fast path disabled
- **Default** — paper's 1/32 + 1/512
- **CBP-static** — per-class atomic counters wired up but adaptation disabled (isolates bookkeeping cost)
- **CBP-v3** — full hill-climber adaptation

## Results

| | TPC-C | Random Read | Skewed YCSB |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional | 636 ± 10 k (−0.1 %) | 15 467 ± 67 k (−4.3 %) | 25 852 ± 482 k (+1.7 %) |
| **Default** | **637 ± 13 k** | **16 153 ± 378 k** | **25 431 ± 274 k** |
| CBP-static | 546 ± 2 k (−14.3 %) | 14 812 ± 5 k (−8.3 %) | 19 630 ± 410 k (−22.8 %) |
| CBP-v3 | 564 ± 16 k (**−11.5 %**) | 14 435 ± 402 k (**−10.6 %**) | 19 740 ± 211 k (**−22.4 %**) |

## Verdict

**Invalidates claim:C3 in its original form** — CBP-v3 is worse than the static default on every workload.

**Supports claim:C2** — bookkeeping floor alone is 8–23 % below default; full adaptation closes ≤ 3 % of that.

End-of-run per-class `log_chance` distribution (TPC-C): mean 8.86, σ 3.53, range [2, 14] — the hill-climber drifted toward "less promote" on a workload where the optimum is at log_chance = 4.
