# E3 — CBP-v4 comparison (round 2)

## Setup

After round-1 review flagged (a) bookkeeping cost and (b) one-sided score function, redesign and re-run with two configurations only. Replicates increased to n=3.

* **Default** — paper's 1/32 + 1/512
* **CBP-v4** — thread-local accumulators (bulk-flush per ~4 K accesses) + 2-arm counterfactual bandit on hit_rate

## Results

| | Default | CBP-v4 | Δ | Within 1σ of default? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TPC-C | 679 ± 18 k TX/s | **673 ± 6 k** | **−0.83 %** | ✅ |
| Random Read | 15 870 ± 570 k | **15 832 ± 229 k** | **−0.23 %** | ✅ |
| Skewed YCSB | 25 885 ± 1 056 k | 23 973 ± 993 k | **−7.39 %** | ❌ |

## Verdict

**Supports claim:C4** — CBP-v4 is statistically indistinguishable from the static default on 2/3 workloads. The thread-local-counter redesign eliminated essentially all of round-1's bookkeeping floor on TPC-C and Random Read.

**Supports claim:C5 (new)** — the bandit overshoots: end-of-run `(lc_lo, lc_hi)` lands at extremes for every workload (TPC-C/rndread drift to (13, 14), ycsb_skewed to (1, 2)). hit_rate is monotonic in log_chance so the bandit never finds the throughput-optimal stopping point.
