# C1 — 1/16 beats 1/32 on TPC-C and Random Read

## Statement

For the in-memory regime at 16 threads, the static promotion chance that maximizes throughput is **1/16** on both TPC-C and uniform random-read, beating the paper's **1/32** default by **+1.2 %** and **+1.6 %** respectively. Skewed YCSB (zipf=0.9), however, peaks at **1/32** — the paper's choice — confirming the default is well-tuned for skewed-read workloads.

## Evidence

* exp:E1 — sensitivity sweep (n=2 reps each)

## Caveats

* TPC-C Δ = +8 k is within one σ (~35 k) — directional, not significant.
* Hardware differs from paper (Intel Xeon vs AMD EPYC).
* 16 threads, not 192. Behavior may flip at higher concurrency.

## Status

**partial** — directional finding consistent across two replicates, but TPC-C delta within 1σ. Would require ≥ 5 reps to escalate to **supported**.
