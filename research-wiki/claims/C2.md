# C2 — CBP-v3 bookkeeping floor is 8–23 %

## Statement

Wiring 64 per-class atomic counters (`atomic<u32>` increments on `total` and `in_pref`, sampled 1-in-8) into the PrediCache access hot path costs **8 % on Random Read, 14 % on TPC-C, and 23 % on Skewed YCSB** throughput — *even with adaptation disabled* (`CBP_STATIC=1`). The bookkeeping floor dominates any plausible adaptation gain.

## Evidence

* exp:E2 — CBP-static row vs Default row.

## Mechanism

At 25 M+ TX/s, the per-access cost of ~3 atomic relaxed adds (cache-line ownership, memory fence implicit on x86) is amplified into a percentage of cycles proportional to throughput. Atomic adds are ≪ 1 ns each but the cache-line ownership cost in concurrent threads makes them effectively expensive.

## Status

**supported** — directly measured by the `CBP_STATIC=1` ablation in E2.

## Implication

Any future per-access instrumentation in a high-throughput buffer manager must use thread-local counters with bulk flush (this is what CBP-v4 implemented to cut the overhead to ~0.2 % on TPC-C/rndread).
