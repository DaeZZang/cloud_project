# C3 — CBP-v3 outperforms static default (INVALIDATED)

## Statement (original)

A per-class hill climber that adapts `log_chance` based on `hit_rate − α · promote_rate` will match or exceed the throughput of PrediCache's static 1/32 default.

## Status

**INVALIDATED** by exp:E2 across all three workloads (TPC-C −11.5 %, Random Read −10.6 %, Skewed YCSB −22.4 %).

## Why it failed

1. **Bookkeeping floor (claim:C2)** — atomic counters alone cost 8–23 % before any adaptation runs.
2. **One-sided score function** — `hit_rate − α · promote_rate` only penalises promotion. There's no reward signal that pushes log_chance *down* when more promotion would lift hit_rate. The controller systematically drifts toward "less promote" regardless of where the optimum sits.
3. **Per-class log_chance distribution dispersed**, indicating random walk on noise rather than convergence (std ≈ 3.8, mean 8.9, optimum at 4 for TPC-C).

## Lesson preserved for future ideation

A correct adaptive promotion policy must:
- Have ≪ 1 ns/access bookkeeping cost (use thread-local counters)
- Use a **two-sided** score signal (e.g., counterfactual A/B comparison)
- Probably bandit on a *throughput proxy* (cycles/access) not hit_rate, because hit_rate is monotonic in log_chance and gives no stopping signal at the throughput optimum
